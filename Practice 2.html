Led Zeppelin are an English rock band formed in 1968 in London, England. Guitarist Jimmy Page and multi-instrumentalist John Paul Jones began collaborating in 1967 and soon decided to embark on their next project. After inviting Hobbestweedle vocalist Robert Plant and drummer John Bonham, they began appearing live and recorded their first studio album in late 1968. Throughout their 12 year career, they released nine albums, went on 28 tours and transformed rock music as we know it. Led Zeppelin began by experiementing with American Blues rock, drawing inspiration from blues legends such as Muddy Waters and John Lee Hooker.
